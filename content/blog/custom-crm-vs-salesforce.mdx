## The $300/Month Question

If you're a small business paying for Salesforce, HubSpot, or Zoho, here's a question worth asking: how much of it do you actually use?

For most small businesses, the answer is somewhere between 15% and 30%. You're paying enterprise prices for a tool designed for enterprise workflows. The dashboards you never open. The integrations you don't need. The 47 custom fields someone set up three years ago that nobody remembers.

Meanwhile, the three things you actually need — tracking leads, sending follow-ups, and knowing which deals are closing — are buried under layers of complexity.

## What a Custom CRM Actually Looks Like

A custom CRM isn't a stripped-down version of Salesforce. It's a purpose-built system designed around how your team actually works.

Here's what that means in practice:

- **Your terminology, not theirs.** If your team calls them "projects" instead of "opportunities," the CRM says "projects." No mental translation required.
- **Your pipeline, not a template.** A roofing contractor's sales process looks nothing like a marketing agency's. Your CRM should reflect that.
- **Your integrations, not a marketplace.** Connect to the specific tools you use — QuickBooks, your email provider, your scheduling system — without paying for a connector plugin.
- **Your brand, your data.** No third-party logos. No vendor lock-in. You own the code and every byte of data.

## The Real Cost Comparison

Let's do the math that SaaS vendors hope you never do.

**Salesforce for a 5-person team:**
- $75/user/month x 5 = $375/month
- Add-ons (email integration, reports, storage): ~$100/month
- Admin/consultant to manage it: ~$200/month
- **Total: ~$675/month = $8,100/year = $40,500 over 5 years**

**Custom CRM:**
- One-time build: $25,000 - $50,000
- Monthly hosting and support: $200-$500/month
- **5-year total: $37,000 - $80,000 — and you own it forever**

At the low end, a custom CRM pays for itself in under 4 years. At the high end, you're getting a system built exactly for your business at roughly the same cost as renting a generic one — except you own it outright.

## When Custom Makes Sense (and When It Doesn't)

Custom CRM is the right move when:

- You're paying $200+/month for tools that are 70% bloat
- Your workflows don't fit into any existing platform's template
- You need to own your data and your codebase
- You want to integrate AI automation directly into your CRM
- Per-seat pricing is killing you as you grow

It might NOT be the right move when:

- You're a solo founder who needs basic contact management
- Your sales process is completely standard with no customization needs
- You're not sure what your process looks like yet (figure it out first, then build)
