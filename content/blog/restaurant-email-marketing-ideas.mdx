## Why Email Marketing Still Works for Restaurants

Social media gets the attention, but email drives the revenue. For restaurants, email marketing delivers an average ROI of $36 for every dollar spent — higher than any other channel. The key is moving beyond generic blasts and sending emails that actually make people want to come eat.

Here are the ideas that work right now.

## Seasonal Menu Launch Campaigns

Every time you update your menu — spring, summer, fall, holiday — that's an email campaign. Not a generic "new menu" announcement, but a story: why you chose these dishes, where the ingredients come from, what inspired the new cocktail list.

Include 2-3 hero photos of the best new dishes. Link to your online ordering or reservation system. Send it Tuesday morning (highest open rates for restaurant emails).

## The "We Miss You" Re-engagement Sequence

This is the highest-ROI email a restaurant can send. When a regular hasn't visited in 3-4 weeks, trigger an automated email:

- Reference their usual order or last visit
- Include a personal offer (free appetizer, 10% off)
- Make it feel human, not robotic

A custom [email marketing system](/services/email-marketing) can trigger these automatically based on actual visit data from your POS — something generic email tools like Mailchimp can't do without manual work.

## Birthday and Anniversary Offers

Collect birthdays at sign-up (just month and day — people don't want to give their year). Send a birthday email 3-5 days before with a compelling offer: free dessert, complimentary drink, or a special prix fixe menu.

These emails have the highest redemption rates of any restaurant campaign — often 30-40% open rates and 15-20% redemption.

## Post-Visit Review Requests

The best time to ask for a review is within 24 hours of a visit. An automated email that says "Thanks for dining with us last night — how was the lamb shank?" with a direct link to your Google Business profile converts at 5-10x the rate of a generic review request.

This requires your email system to know who visited and when — another reason why a [custom CRM](/services/custom-crm) integrated with your POS beats standalone email tools.

## Loyalty Program Updates

If you have a loyalty program (and you should), email is how you keep members engaged:

- Monthly points balance statements
- "You're 50 points from your next reward" nudges
- Exclusive member-only offers
- Early access to events and seasonal menus

A custom [loyalty platform](/industries/restaurants) makes this automatic — every visit earns points, every milestone triggers an email.

## Event and Special Night Promotions

Wine dinners, live music nights, holiday brunches, chef's table experiences — these are perfect email content. Send dedicated event emails 2 weeks out, then a reminder 3 days before.

Pro tip: segment your list. Send wine dinner emails to customers who've ordered wine before. Send brunch emails to weekend visitors. The more targeted, the higher the conversion.

## The Weekly Special or "Chef's Pick"

A simple weekly email featuring one dish, one drink, or one special. Keep it short — one great photo, two sentences, a link to order or reserve. This builds a habit of opening your emails because there's always something new and specific.

## What Makes Restaurant Email Marketing Actually Work

The ideas above are only as good as the system behind them. If you're manually exporting customer lists from your POS, importing them into Mailchimp, and building campaigns from scratch — you'll burn out and stop doing it.

The restaurants that win at email marketing have systems where:

- Emails trigger automatically based on behavior (visit frequency, spending, preferences)
- Customer data flows from POS to email without manual work
- Campaigns are personalized with actual order history
- Results are tracked back to revenue, not just open rates
