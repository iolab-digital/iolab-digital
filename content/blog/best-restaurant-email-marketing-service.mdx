## Restaurant Email Marketing Is Different

Restaurants aren't selling software subscriptions or e-commerce products. You're selling experiences — a Tuesday night dinner, a birthday celebration, a new seasonal menu. Your email marketing needs to reflect that, and most generic email platforms don't understand the rhythm of the restaurant business.

Here's what actually works for restaurant email marketing in 2026, from budget-friendly to best-in-class.

## The Best Platforms for Restaurant Email

### Toast Marketing Suite

If you're already on Toast for POS, their built-in marketing tools are the path of least resistance. Campaign data ties directly to your transaction history, which means you can target customers based on what they've actually ordered and spent.

**Pricing:** $75/month add-on to your Toast subscription.

**Best for:** Toast POS users who want basic email and SMS without adding another platform.

**Limitations:** Template options are limited. Automation is basic (birthday emails, lapsed customer reminders). You're locked into the Toast ecosystem. Can't integrate with non-Toast tools.

### Mailchimp

Still the most popular option for restaurants new to email marketing. The free tier and drag-and-drop builder make it accessible, and the restaurant-specific templates help you get started.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 500 contacts, $13-$350/month.

**Best for:** Single-location restaurants with small lists who want to send occasional newsletters and promotions.

**Limitations:** No POS integration. No reservation data. Customer data has to be manually imported or synced through third-party tools. Per-subscriber pricing means your cost grows as your list grows.

### Constant Contact

An older platform that's still popular with local restaurants. Solid deliverability, decent templates, and hands-on customer support (actual phone support, which is rare).

**Pricing:** $12-$80/month.

**Best for:** Restaurant owners who want human support when they get stuck, not a chatbot.

**Limitations:** The interface feels dated. Automation features are behind competitors. Analytics are basic.

### BentoBox Marketing

BentoBox (owned by Fiserv) builds restaurant websites and includes email marketing tools designed specifically for hospitality. Their templates are restaurant-focused and their data integrates with reservations and events.

**Pricing:** Part of BentoBox website packages (varies, typically $99-$199/month total).

**Best for:** Restaurants already using BentoBox for their website who want everything in one ecosystem.

**Limitations:** Tied to BentoBox's website platform. Switching costs are high. Automation is basic.

## What Restaurants Actually Need from Email Marketing

Most restaurant email "best practices" lists focus on the same generic advice: send a weekly newsletter, promote specials, collect emails at the door. That's table stakes. Here's what actually moves the needle:

- **Guest-behavior-triggered emails** — not scheduled blasts, but emails triggered by what a customer actually does (hasn't visited in 30 days, ordered a specific dish, attended an event)
- **Reservation-integrated sequences** — confirmation, pre-visit menu preview, post-visit thank-you with review request
- **Seasonal automation** — campaigns that fire automatically when seasonal menus launch, holiday bookings open, or special events are announced
- **Loyalty integration** — email tied to a [rewards program](/industries/restaurants) where points, perks, and status drive engagement

None of the platforms above do all of this out of the box.

This is where a custom-built email system shines. When your [email marketing](/services/email-marketing) is integrated with your [CRM](/services/custom-crm), reservation system, POS, and loyalty program, you get:

- Emails that reference a guest's actual order history
- Automated re-engagement when regulars haven't visited
- Birthday and anniversary campaigns with personalized offers
- Post-dining review requests timed to peak satisfaction
- Zero per-subscriber fees — your cost doesn't grow with your list

The investment is higher upfront ($15,000-$25,000), but you own the system, the templates, and every piece of customer data. And with [AI automation](/services/ai-automation), the system writes and sends most emails without human involvement.
